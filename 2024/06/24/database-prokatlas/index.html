MetaMetaDB

Currently, MetaMetaDB contains 2,949,852 representative 16S rRNA sequences from 61 diverse environments. (data by November 6, 2014)

Usage

Example: Hiraoka, S., Miyahara, M., Fujii, K., Machiyama, A. and Iwasaki, W., 2017. Seasonal analysis of microbial communities in precipitation in the greater Tokyo area, Japan. Frontiers in microbiology, 8, p.1506. https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2017.01506

“To estimate ordinary habitats for each 16S rRNA sequence, a BLASTN search was performed against MetaMetaDB (Yang and Iwasaki, 2014), and the top hit sequence with an e-value ≤ 1E-10 and an identity≥ 90% was retrieved. Microbial habitability index (MHI) scores were calculated as previously described (Yang and Iwasaki, 2014).”

“To estimate the environments from which microbes in precipitation originated, we performed a microbial habitat index analysis using MetaMetaDB (Yang and Iwasaki, 2014), which is a database to estimate the ordinary habitats of microbes based on similarity searches for 16S rRNA gene sequences against amplicon-sequencing and shotgun metagenomic data in public databases.”

Updates - ProkAtlas

  • online version: https://msk33.github.io/prokatlas.html

  • ProkAtlas database: http://msk33.github.io/ProkAtlas.fa.zip

  • Source code for calculating microbial habitat preference scores (This script is written in python and requires pandas)

Usage

Example: Yokoyama, D. and Kikuchi, J., 2023. Inferring microbial community assembly in an urban river basin through geo-multi-omics and phylogenetic bin-based null-model analysis of surface water. Environmental Research, 231, p.116202. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.envres.2023.116202

“For additional analysis, habitats were predicted under the scheme of ProkAtlas (Mise and Iwasaki, 2020) where the microbial habitats were predicted from 16 S rRNA sequences with reference to a database linking shotgun metagenome sequences to the collected habitats. The ASV table was processed to generate the proportions of 115 preferential habitats, which were categorized as follows: ocean-related, freshwater-related, soil-related, wastewater and human–related, and other habitats (Table S3).”

“According to ProkAtlas analysis, the microbiomes showed clear preferential habitat patterns. With a few exceptions, the proportion of ocean-related microbes was high in estuary environments, whereas the proportion of freshwater-related microbes was high in upper stream environments (Fig. S7).”

“The microbial preference to salinity is also confirmed by habitat prediction of ProkAtlas, where the proportions of ocean-related sequences in FL microbes were elevated in estuary environments while the proportions of freshwater-related sequences were higher in upper streams (Fig. S7). This analysis predicts the optimal environments for microbiomes based on a wide range of sequence–habitat data and provides a robust interpretation of environmental selection. The relationship between the predicted habitats and geography adds to the strength of this interpretation.”
